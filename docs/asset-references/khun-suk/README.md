# Khun Suk — moderation-safe reference assets

These are **reference-only** source files for regenerating the Khun Suk (Muay Thai)
mascot art. They are **not** shipped in the app and must **not** be imported by any
component or wired into the product UI. They live here so the approved look-and-feel
survives in version control until a moderation-safe Champion art pack can be
regenerated by the approved generation workflow.

## Background

The original Champion art pack (`/public/characters/muay-thai-champion/`) and the
two Khun Suk cinematics (`stage-3.mp4`, `course-complete.mp4`) were **rejected**.
The original homepage Muay Thai art (`/public/characters/muay-thai/idle.webp`) shows
a bare-chested child, which the image generator's input filter rejects. The
moderation-safe reference below clothes the torso with a red sleeveless top while
preserving the face, eyes, hairstyle, headband (mongkol), skin tone, body
proportions, shorts, and 3D style.

## Current temporary state (pending regeneration)

While the Champion pack is disabled, the product borrows the **approved base
`muay-thai` art pack** for Khun Suk:

- **Stage 8 coach** keeps its champion identity, name, voice copy, and sound, but
  renders the approved base art (`muay-thai-champion` character `artId` → `muay-thai`
  in `src/data/characters.js`).
- **Stage 3 coach** already uses the approved base `muay-thai` pack.
- **Stage 3 and course-complete cinematics are disabled** in
  `src/data/stageCinematics.js`; the completion flow falls back to the existing
  static celebration overlay.

To restore the Champion experience once a moderation-safe pack is generated:

1. Drop the seven approved expression WebPs into `/public/characters/muay-thai-champion/`.
2. Flip `artId` back to `'muay-thai-champion'` in `src/data/characters.js` and
   `src/data/stageCharacters.js`.
3. Re-add the Stage 3 and Stage 8 entries in `src/data/stageCinematics.js` once the
   approved clips ship.
