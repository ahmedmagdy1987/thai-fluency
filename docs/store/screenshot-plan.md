# Screenshot Plan - Tuk Talk Thai

> Status: DRAFT for owner review. Capture from a real device build (Android debug
> APK or a tooled emulator; iOS once an iOS build exists). Keep captions short and
> strong. Do not overclaim. Use the brand dark-green header (#0F3D2E) and the cream
> background (#F5F0E5). Hide personal data and use a clean status bar.

## How to capture cleanly
- Use a fresh demo account or a seeded progress state so streaks, XP, and completed
  units look real but not empty.
- Android: `adb shell screencap -p /sdcard/shot.png` then `adb pull` (do not use
  shell redirection, it corrupts PNGs).
- iOS: use the Simulator screenshot once an iOS build exists.
- Add device frames and the caption as an overlay band; do not bake text into the
  app UI.

## The 8 screenshots

### 1. Landing / value proposition
- Screen: the public landing / welcome screen ("Real Thai for real life.").
- Caption: **Real Thai for real life**
- Why it matters: the first frame sets positioning; speak-first, practical Thai.
- Device frame: phone in a clean modern frame (Pixel 8 for Android, iPhone 15 Pro for iOS).
- Size notes: this is the hero shot; place it first in both stores.

### 2. Guided path with stages
- Screen: the Learn path showing the 8-stage journey with the current stage active.
- Caption: **A clear path across 8 stages**
- Why it matters: shows structure and progress, the main reason people stick with it.
- Device frame: same phone frame; show 2 to 3 stage rows and the active one.
- Size notes: ensure the stage labels are legible at thumbnail size.

### 3. Mini-unit lesson
- Screen: a mini-unit in progress (intro or vocab step) with a Thai phrase, phonetics, and English.
- Caption: **Short lessons that fit your day**
- Why it matters: proves the bite-size, low-pressure learning loop.
- Device frame: phone frame; show the card with phonetics and English visible.
- Size notes: keep Thai script readable; avoid clutter.

### 4. Sentence Builder
- Screen: the tap-to-build Sentence Builder mid-task with word tiles.
- Caption: **Build real Thai sentences, tap by tap**
- Why it matters: a distinctive, interactive feature that teaches word order.
- Device frame: phone frame; show a partially built sentence and the tile tray.
- Size notes: show the feedback state (correct tile placement) for clarity.

### 5. Stage Challenge
- Screen: a Stage Challenge question with answer options.
- Caption: **Test what you know, both ways**
- Why it matters: signals active recall and Thai to English plus English to Thai practice.
- Device frame: phone frame; show the question and four options.
- Size notes: pick a clear, common phrase so it reads instantly.

### 6. Quests / streaks
- Screen: the daily Quests screen with streak and quest progress.
- Caption: **Daily quests keep your streak alive**
- Why it matters: shows the motivation loop that drives daily habit.
- Device frame: phone frame; show the streak flame and a couple of quests.
- Size notes: a small but non-zero streak number reads better than zero.

### 7. Celebration / achievement
- Screen: a celebration overlay or achievement-unlocked modal with XP count-up.
- Caption: **Celebrate every win**
- Why it matters: communicates that practice feels rewarding, not a chore.
- Device frame: phone frame; capture the confetti or badge moment.
- Size notes: ensure the overlay fits the screen with no clipping.

### 8. Course progress
- Screen: the course-progress / course-complete banner on the Learn path.
- Caption: **See how far you have come**
- Why it matters: shows the long-term payoff and a sense of accomplishment.
- Device frame: phone frame; show the progress summary (stages, mini-units).
- Size notes: good closing frame for the carousel.

## Device sizes and store requirements

### Google Play (Android)
- Phone screenshots: 2 to 8 images, PNG or JPEG, minimum 1080 px on the shorter
  side. Recommended 1080 x 1920 or 1080 x 2400 (portrait).
- Feature graphic: 1024 x 500 px (required for the listing).
- App icon: 512 x 512 px, 32-bit PNG.
- Optional 7-inch and 10-inch tablet screenshots if tablet support is declared.

### App Store (iOS) - for when an iOS build exists
- iPhone 6.7-inch display (required): 1290 x 2796 px portrait.
- iPhone 6.5-inch display (optional, legacy): 1242 x 2688 px portrait.
- iPad 12.9-inch display (only if iPad is supported): 2048 x 2732 px portrait.
- App icon: 1024 x 1024 px, no alpha, no rounded corners (Apple rounds it).

### Shared guidance
- Keep the same 8-shot order in both stores for a consistent story.
- First 2 to 3 shots matter most; lead with landing, guided path, and a lesson.
- Captions stay under about 6 words so they read at thumbnail size.
