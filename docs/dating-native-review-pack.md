## How to review

For each phrase, please check:

1. **Accuracy** — is the Thai correct and natural for the stated English meaning?
2. **Naturalness** — would a Thai adult actually say this, in this form, today?
3. **Romanization & tones** — do the phonetics (marks: à low · á high · â falling · ǎ rising · none mid) match?
4. **Tone/severity label** — the app maps severity → learner guidance (gentle → “Safe to use”, moderate → “Use carefully”, strong → recognition-only, safety → consent/safety language). Is the label right?
5. **Gender/particles** — phrases ship male-polite form (ผม / ครับ); the app tells female speakers to swap (ค่ะ / ฉัน). Is that swap valid here?
6. **Culture/safety framing** — is the context note fair, and are warnings adequate for anything rude or risky?

Fill the verdict line under each phrase: check ONE box, and use the notes line for corrections (corrected Thai/phonetics welcome).
